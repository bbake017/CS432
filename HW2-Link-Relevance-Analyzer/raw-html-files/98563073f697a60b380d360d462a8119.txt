<p>President Trump kicked off a slew of executive actions related to immigration in a signing ceremony at the Oval Office.</p> <p>Since the early days of the 2024 presidential campaign, Trump has vowed to begin his second term with both new and old efforts to curb legal migration and deport those who are in the U.S. without legal status.</p> <p>He signed a series of actions related to the border on Monday, including "realigning" the Refugee Admission Program, designating cartels as foreign terrorist organization, declaring a national emergency at the southern border, and ending automatic citizenship to children born in the U.S. to parents who don't have legal status  a controversial constitutional question that is likely to face immediate legal challenges.</p> <aside aria-label="advertisement" class="ad-wrap backstage" id="ad-backstage-wrap">

<p>The U.S. had seen an increase in border crossings under the Biden administration, <a href="https://www.npr.org/2023/11/12/1212058889/migrants-u-s-southern-border-historic-numbers-why" target="_blank">at times reaching all-time highs</a>. But Customs and Border Protection's <a href="https://www.npr.org/2025/01/15/nx-s1-5258476/trump-border-crossings-immigration-u-s-mexico" target="_blank">recent numbers have shown a sharp decrease</a> in unauthorized apprehensions in the past six months.</p> <p>Incoming Trump border czar Tom Homan <a href="https://www.npr.org/2025/01/18/nx-s1-5266892/immigration-raid-chicago-trump" target="_blank">also said</a> large-scale raids to deport and detain those without legal status are set to begin as soon as Tuesday, focusing on people considered a security or safety threat.<br/></p> <h3 class="edTag">Here are the executive actions that officials previewed:</h3> <p><strong>1.Declare a </strong><a href="https://www.whitehouse.gov/presidential-actions/2025/01/declaring-a-national-emergency-at-the-southern-border-of-the-united-states/" target="_blank"><strong>national emergency</strong></a><strong> at the border</strong></p> <p>The order will allow U.S. armed forces to finish the border wall, by directing the defense and homeland security secretaries to erect physical barriers at the border. It will also allow the defense secretary to deploy members of the armed forces and National Guard to the border.</p> <p><strong>2.Clarify the </strong><a href="https://www.whitehouse.gov/presidential-actions/2025/01/clarifying-the-militarys-role-in-protecting-the-territorial-integrity-of-the-united-states/" target="_blank"><strong>military's role</strong></a><strong> when it comes to border</strong></p> <p>The order seeks to "clarify" the military's role in protecting the territorial integrity of the United States, adding that "it is the policy of the United States to ensure that the Armed Forces of the United States prioritize the protection of the sovereignty and territorial integrity of the United States along our national borders."</p> <aside aria-label="advertisement" class="ad-wrap secondary" id="ad-secondary-wrap">

<p><strong>3.Secure </strong><a href="https://www.whitehouse.gov/presidential-actions/2025/01/securing-our-borders/" target="_blank"><strong>the border</strong></a></p> <p>Officials said they're also planning to end the policy known as "catch and release," which released those without legal status from detention while they awaited an immigration court hearing.</p> <p>The action would also reinstate "Remain in Mexico," which would require some asylum seekers as the southern border to wait in Mexico for their hearings in U.S. immigration court. And it would direct the U.S. to build the wall along the southern border.</p> <p><strong>4.Designate criminal cartels </strong><a href="https://www.whitehouse.gov/presidential-actions/2025/01/protecting-the-united-states-from-foreign-terrorists-and-othernational-security-and-public-safety-threats/" target="_blank"><strong>as terrorists</strong></a></p> <p>The Trump White House designated criminal cartels and others as foreign terrorist organizations and as specifically designated global terrorists  two separate legal definitions. This allows the U.S. to more easily remove members of groups like the Tren de Aragua, a transnational criminal organization from Venezuela, and MS-13.</p> <p>The order is also meant to "enhance vetting and screening" across agencies, which White House officials had earlier mentioned as a separate action.</p> <p><strong>5.Suspend </strong><a href="https://www.whitehouse.gov/presidential-actions/2025/01/realigning-the-united-states-refugee-admissions-program/" target="_blank"><strong>refugee resettlement</strong></a></p> <p>The order temporarily suspends the U.S. Refugee Admissions Program starting from Jan. 27, "until such time as the further entry into the United States of refugees aligns with the interests of the United States."</p> <p><strong>6.End asylum and close the border to those without legal status via proclamation</strong></p> <p>White House officials, on a call with reporters earlier on Monday, also said they are planning to end asylum entirely and close the border to those without legal status via proclamation, "which creates an immediate removal process without possibility of asylum." The officials spoke on condition of anonymity to preview the actions. Trump earlier on Monday <a href="https://www.whitehouse.gov/presidential-actions/2025/01/initial-rescissions-of-harmful-executive-orders-and-actions/" target="_blank">rescinded a Biden</a><a href="https://www.uscis.gov/sites/default/files/document/foia/ExecutiveOrder14010-Sullivan.pdf" target="_blank">order</a> on asylum seekers.</p> <p><strong>7.End </strong><a href="https://www.whitehouse.gov/presidential-actions/2025/01/protecting-the-meaning-and-value-of-american-citizenship/" target="_blank"><strong>birthright citizenship</strong></a></p> <p>The order aims to end <a href="https://www.npr.org/sections/thetwo-way/2015/08/18/432707866/3-things-you-should-know-about-birthright-citizenship">birthright citizenship</a>, which is enshrined in the 14th Amendment to the U.S. Constitution since 1868 and provides the right to citizenship for children born in the U.S., regardless of their parents' legal status. In an interpretation of the amendment's clause of "subject to the jurisdiction thereof," the order, which takes effect in 30 days, would narrow who can get U.S. citizenship based on the legal status of their parents.</p> <aside aria-label="advertisement" class="ad-wrap third" id="ad-third-wrap">

<p>This action to change the interpretation of the amendment is likely to see immediate legal challenges.</p> <p><strong>8.Guarantee </strong><a href="https://www.whitehouse.gov/presidential-actions/2025/01/guaranteeing-the-states-protection-against-invasion/" target="_blank"><strong>states protection against invasion</strong></a></p> <p>The proclamation Trump issued is "suspending the physical entry of aliens involved in an invasion into the United States across the southern border until I determine that the invasion has concluded," according to the text.</p> <p><strong>9."Protect </strong><a href="https://www.whitehouse.gov/presidential-actions/2025/01/protecting-the-american-people-against-invasion/" target="_blank"><strong>American citizens against invasion</strong></a><strong>"</strong></p> <p>"What this particular action does is it rescinds the open borders policies of the Biden administration and equips agents and officers of ICE and CBP with the authorities" they need to deport people from the U.S. "It also reaffirms the faithful execution of the immigration laws, prioritizes enforcement actions against criminal illegal aliens and establishes federal Homeland Security Task Forces to cooperate with state and local law enforcement in the removal of gangs, criminals and illegal aliens from the United States," White House officials told reporters on a call previewing the move earlier on Monday.</p> <p>The latest <a href="https://www.npr.org/2025/01/15/nx-s1-5259893/poll-trump-immigration-economy-favorability">NPR/PBS News/Marist poll</a> also finds that Americans are evenly split on whether to mass deport people who are in the U.S. without legal status  though divisions fall along party lines. There are also concerns about the practical impact of deportations, including <a href="https://www.npr.org/2025/01/16/nx-s1-5218641/immigrant-detention-trump-deportation-plans">where to house</a> millions of detainees while they await deportation.</p> <p><strong>10. "Restore" the </strong><a href="https://www.whitehouse.gov/presidential-actions/2025/01/restoring-the-death-penalty-and-protecting-public-safety/" target="_blank"><strong>death penalty</strong></a></p> <p>This action directs the attorney general to seek capital punishment for the murder of law enforcement officers and capital crimes committed by those without legal status.</p> <p>"So as you know, this is about national security, this is about public safety, and this is about the victims of some of the most violent, abusive criminals we've seen enter our country in our lifetime. And it ends today," the officials told reporters on the preview call.</p> <p>Capital punishment is legal at the federal level, and President Biden had pledged to abolish it  though he never worked with Congress on legislation to do so. Biden did <a href="https://www.npr.org/2024/12/23/g-s1-38794/biden-death-row-commutations">commute the sentences</a> of 37 of the 40 men on federal death row to life without parole last month.</p> <aside aria-label="advertisement" class="ad-wrap overflow-3" id="ad-overflow-3-wrap">

<p>In fact, even quickly scaling operations might be difficult for the new administration. <a href="https://www.npr.org/2024/08/14/nx-s1-5037992/trump-immigrants-border-mass-deportation-presidential-race-migrants" target="_blank">An NPR investigation last year</a> found that U.S. Immigrations and Customs Enforcement, the agency responsible for removals, struggled to scale up to Trump's immediate demands during his first term, which included attempts to increase deportations.</p> <p>Congress must also provide the Department of Homeland Security and other agencies with the funding to execute the policies.</p> <p>In their final budget request, the Biden administration asked for $19 billion to fund additional personnel, facilities, repatriation capabilities and other <a href="https://www.dhs.gov/news/2024/03/11/statement-secretary-mayorkas-presidents-fiscal-year-2025-budget-us-department" target="_blank">enforcement resources along the southwest border</a>.</p> <p>Lawmakers are expected to take up border security funding as a part of a bigger budget-related measure later this year.<br/> </p>
