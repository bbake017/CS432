<div class="i9-c-rich-text-widget col-12 col-md-6 col-lg-8">
<p>
Official U.S. data go back to 1913 for a consumer price index (CPI) comparable to what the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS) still calculates today. You can use the Minneapolis Feds inflation calculator to instantly compare the buying power of past and present dollars back to 1913. </p>
<p>The table below reflects the following historical series as initially compiled by the BLS for the <a href="https://fraser.stlouisfed.org/title/handbook-labor-statistics-4025?browse=1970s" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">Handbook of Labor Statistics</a>, with modern CPI data from 1913 to the present day:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>1800 to 1851</strong> - Index of Prices Paid by Vermont Farmers for Family Living</li>
<li><strong>1851 to 1890</strong> - Consumer Price Index by Ethel D. Hoover</li>
<li><strong>1890 to 1912</strong> - Cost of Living Index by Albert Rees </li>
<li><strong>1913 to 1977</strong> - Consumer Price Index (CPI)</li>
<li><strong>1978 to present</strong> - Consumer Price Index for all Urban Consumers (CPI-U)</li>
</ul>
</div>
