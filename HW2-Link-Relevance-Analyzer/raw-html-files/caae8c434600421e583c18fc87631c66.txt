By saving information in a cookie, websites and servers can remember preferences or recognize web browsers from one visit to another. </li><li><strong>Cross-Device Graph</strong>: A map of pseudonymous identifiers that are determined to likely belong to the same user across multiple devices, such as a laptop, smartphone, and tablet. </li><li><strong>ID Syncing</strong>: A common process in the digital advertising industry that enables advertisers to link up pseudonymous data sets from multiple advertising platforms. </li><li><strong>Interest-Based Advertising</strong>: Associates a user's activity and interest information with a browser cookie or other online identifier in order to provide more useful and relevant advertising and/or content. It is sometimes also called “ad targeting” "online behavioral advertising", "targeted advertising", "cross-context behavioral advertising" or "tailored advertising". </li><li><strong>Mobile Advertising Identifiers</strong>: Unique IDs associated with individual mobile devices (e.g., Apple's IDFA or Google's AAID). </li><li><strong>Persistent Identifier</strong>: An identifier that is stored on a user's device or otherwise associated with a user over an extended period. This allows a browser or device to be recognized across different Browser sessions or over time. Common examples of persistent identifiers include cookies and mobile advertising identifiers. </li><li><strong>Probabilistic Identification</strong>: The process of using a combination of pseudonymous signals passively made available by a device or browser (such as operating system, browser type, IP address, and user agent) to infer a likely match to an existing ID5 ID without the use of cookies or other persistent identifiers stored on the device. </li><li><strong>Profiling</strong>: Any form of automated processing of Personal Data to evaluate, analyze, or predict personal aspects concerning an individual. </li></ul><h2 class="scroll-mt-20" id="what-information-do-we-collect-and-use"> What Information Do We Collect? </h2><ul><li><strong>Device Data</strong>.
