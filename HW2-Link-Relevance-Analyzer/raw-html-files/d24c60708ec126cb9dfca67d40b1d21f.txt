<p>Before Donald Trump took the oath of office one year ago, the presidency was widely seen as an all-consuming, full-time job.</p> <p>But since Inauguration Day on Jan. 20, 2017, <a href="https://www.npr.org/2017/02/17/513724796/trump-ethics-monitor-has-the-president-kept-his-promises">Trump has served as president while holding on</a> to his hundreds of businesses. He has continued to promote his Trump-branded properties, spending <a href="https://www.wsj.com/articles/president-trump-spent-nearly-one-third-of-first-year-in-office-at-trump-owned-properties-1514206800">roughly a third of his presidency</a> dining at his own restaurants, golfing at his own courses and sleeping at his own luxury hotels and resorts.</p> <p>As Trump has often pointed out, a <a href="https://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/208">law setting ethical standards</a> regarding self-enrichment applies to federal employees but exempts the president and vice president. "The law is totally on my side, meaning, the president can't have a conflict of interest," <a href="https://www.nytimes.com/2016/11/23/us/politics/trump-new-york-times-interview-transcript.html">he told The New York Times</a> shortly after the election.</p> <aside aria-label="advertisement" class="ad-wrap backstage" id="ad-backstage-wrap">

<p>By underscoring that one legal exemption, Trump has called attention to this political reality: Ethical norms for the presidency are just that — norms, not legal requirements. In the modern era, previous presidents have sold off their holdings or put them into professionally managed blind trusts to avoid conflicts of interests. But they did so by choice.</p> <div class="bucketwrap internallink insettwocolumn inset2col" id="res579297615">

<p>Over the past year, Trump has shown that such traditions can be ignored. Now, when celebrities, such as <a href="https://www.npr.org/2018/01/08/576472393/oprah-2020-people-are-asking-after-golden-globe-speech">Oprah Winfrey</a>, <a href="https://www.cnbc.com/2017/12/19/the-rock-reveals-why-hes-considering-running-for-president.html">Dwayne Johnson, aka "The Rock" </a>or <em>Shark Tank</em> judge <a href="http://fortune.com/2017/11/10/mark-cuban-president-donald-trump/">Mark Cuban</a>, are floated as possible presidential candidates, not much is said about their need to divest from brands and businesses.</p> <p>"We have become inured to the conflicts of interest that continue with Trump's business holdings," Washington ethics lawyer <a href="https://www.skadden.com/professionals/kenneth-gross">Ken Gross</a> said. "We've been overwhelmed by so many other events with this administration that we've put the ethics issue on the middle burner," rather than a front burner, he added.</p> <div class="bucketwrap internallink insettwocolumn inset2col" id="res579296671">

<p>At a press conference on Jan. 11, 2017, Trump lawyer <a href="https://www.nytimes.com/2017/01/11/us/politics/trump-press-conference-transcript.html">Sheri Dillon confirmed that</a>, saying: "You cannot have a totally blind trust with operating businesses. President Trump can't unknow he owns Trump Tower."</p> <p>The Trump Organization also continues trying to expand the family business, seeking and <a href="https://www.nytimes.com/2017/06/13/business/trump-china-trademarks.html">accepting new trademarks</a> in China. Trump's sons <a href="https://www.npr.org/sections/parallels/2018/01/15/577326163/after-a-year-in-office-questions-about-trumps-foreign-deals-go-on-and-on">still pursue business expansions</a> around the globe.</p> <p>Trump has been hit with one lawsuit after another as various entities have tried to prevent him from potentially enriching himself while in office. But Trump the businessman remains inseparable from Trump the president because demands for change have not resulted in any legal requirement to divest, use a blind trust or limit the ways he might be enriched by his businesses while in office.</p> <aside aria-label="advertisement" class="ad-wrap secondary" id="ad-secondary-wrap">
